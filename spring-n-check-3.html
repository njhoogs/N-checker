Soil temp interpolated to 10cm from Open-Meteo's 6cm/18cm modelled layers, using the daily mean (24h average) rather than a single reading. Growth-start trigger follows Frame's rule: ryegrass begins growing once soil temp holds ≥5.5°C for 5 consecutive days. Accumulated growing degree days (base 5°C, from 1 June) are shown as a secondary indicator of growth strength — the 20kgDM/ha/day threshold is a starting estimate that should be calibrated against your own farm-walk data over a season. Rain forecast is summed over the next 7 days from yr.no (MET Norway) where the browser can reach it, falling back to Open-Meteo's ECMWF model otherwise. Soil moisture is depth-weighted across Open-Meteo's modelled layers to represent a 300mm root zone (the conservative end of ryegrass's typical 300-400mm root depth), then converted into a remaining buffer (mm) before the soil reaches field capacity (assumed 30% VWC for Canterbury's typical shallow stony soils — adjust if you have better local data). The rain threshold used to decide "go/hold off" is that buffer plus a small safety margin, rather than a flat number — so a drier soil tolerates more forecast rain, and a wetter soil triggers hold-off sooner. The deficit projection chart shows that buffer falling as forecast rain "fills" it day by day, without accounting for evapotranspiration pulling it back up — a reasonable simplification over a 7-day window, but it can understate the deficit during a dry, sunny spell. Modelled data — treat as a guide alongside what you see and feel in the paddock.
