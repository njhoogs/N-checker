Threshold Comparison

Tap or drag the pin to set the exact location.

Soil temp interpolated to 10cm from Open-Meteo's modelled 0-7cm/7-28cm depth bands (historical archive), averaged across the last 10 years for each calendar date from 1 June to 30 September. Six rules are compared on the same averaged data: (1) 200 growing degree days from 1 June, base 5°C, using daily mean; (2) Frame's rule — daily mean ≥5.5°C for 5 consecutive days, searched only from 1 July onward; (3) 9am reading ≥6°C for 7 consecutive days; (4) 9am reading ≥7°C for 7 consecutive days; (5) and (6) the same 6°C/7°C 9am rules but additionally requiring the window to be net rising (day 7 higher than day 1). This is a pure climatology view averaging temperatures first, then checking thresholds against that averaged curve — a different (and typically smoother) approach than averaging 10 individual years' own crossing dates together. No current-year data, no rain, no soil moisture, no live decision — this complements the main checker tools, it isn't a replacement for them.
